FAQs

Frequently Ask Questions

Choosing the right watch involves considering several factors. First, think about your style preferences. Do you prefer a classic, sporty, or modern look? Next, consider the occasions you'll wear the watch for. A formal event may require a different style than an everyday watch. Lastly, think about any specific features you need, such as water resistance, chronograph (stopwatch), or additional complications like a moon phase or day/date indicator.

It is recommended to service your watch every 3-5 years. During a service, a watchmaker will disassemble the watch, clean and lubricate the movement, check for any worn or damaged parts, and make any necessary adjustments to ensure the watch continues to function properly and maintain its accuracy.

Water-resistant means the watch can withstand some exposure to water, but it is not fully sealed against water entry. Waterproof means the watch is fully sealed and can be submerged to a certain depth without water entering the case. It is important to note that no watch is completely waterproof and should not be exposed to water beyond its rated depth.

Yes, most watches come with a warranty that covers manufacturing defects. The length and terms of the warranty can vary depending on the brand and model of the watch. It is important to read the warranty terms and conditions carefully to understand what is covered and for how long.
